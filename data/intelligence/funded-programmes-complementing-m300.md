# Sierra Leone — Funded Programs Complementing Mission 300 Energy Projects
## Deep Research Report | July 2026

**Prepared for:** Compact Delivery & Monitoring Unit, Ministry of Energy
**Scope:** Active and pipeline-funded programmes across Energy, Agriculture, Digital Transformation, Industrialisation, and Mining that create demand for, or are enabled by, the M300 energy infrastructure portfolio
**Sources:** World Bank, AfDB, IFAD, IFC, UNIDO, UNCDF, WARDIP, ITC, Government Budget Speeches, EITI, AFC, Afreximbank, industry sources (July 2026)

---

## Executive Summary

Sierra Leone's M300 Energy Compact does not sit in isolation. It is the enabling foundation beneath a $3.5+ billion development programme stack that spans Feed Salone (agriculture), the Digital Transformation Project, the ARISE Industrial Zone, UNIDO's industrialisation agenda, and a mining sector renaissance anchored by the $430 million Baomahun Gold Project. Every programme in this report either **creates electricity demand that justifies M300 investment**, **requires reliable energy to deliver its outputs**, or **can co-finance or de-risk M300 infrastructure** through shared investment in productive use zones.

The strategic insight for the CDMU is this: M300 is not simply an energy access programme — it is the infrastructure backbone of Sierra Leone's entire MTNDP 2024–2030 development agenda. Making this cross-sectoral linkage explicit, and building coordination bridges between the energy compact and these programmes, is the most powerful argument for accelerating M300 financing and delivery.

## 1. ENERGY SECTOR — Existing & Pipeline-Funded Programmes

### 1.1 Regional DARES — $60 Million (World Bank IDA, approved July 2026)
The Regional Distributed Access through Renewable Energy Scale-up programme. US$60 million for Sierra Leone under the first phase, part of a broader US$200 million regional package shared with Benin, the Central African Republic, and Liberia, representing the opening phase of a wider US$853 million programme.

### 1.2 ESLEAP — $130 Million (World Bank IDA, 2021–2026 extended)
The Enhancing Sierra Leone Energy Access Project. Components: 1.3 MW Newton solar PV plant; 10 MW Lungi solar PV plant; 33/11kV distribution lines; solar for 296 public facilities; mini-grid construction at 9 new locations; Kingtom substation upgrade.

### 1.3 CLSG Interconnector Programme (Phase 2 Expansion)
1,303 km 225kV transmission line connecting Côte d'Ivoire, Guinea, Liberia, and Sierra Leone. 2.8 million people served, 13.8 million tons CO2 reduced.

### 1.4 WAPP Regional Energy Programme
4,000+ km of 225–330 kV transmission lines connecting sub-Saharan countries from Senegal to Nigeria, benefiting over 18 million people.

## 5. MINING — Funded Programmes Driving Electricity Demand

### 5.1 Baomahun Gold Project — $430 Million (AFC + Afreximbank)
21 MW thermal + 23.8 MW solar PV + 13.8 MWh BESS. 90% renewable during daylight. 10% of GDP.

### 5.2 Sierra Rutile — World's largest natural rutile producer
136,000 tonnes (2022). Sembehun expansion: 173.7M tonnes reserves.

### 5.3 Marampa Iron Ore — Kingho Mining
65% iron "Marampa Blue" concentrate. Pellet plant needs 15-30 MW.

### 5.4 Tonkolili Iron Ore — CRSG
13.7 billion tonnes. 30-50 MW demand at full operation.

### 5.5 Bauxite — Sierra Minerals Holdings
910,000 tonnes (2022). No alumina refinery due to limited power supply.

### 5.6 Artisanal & Small-Scale Mining (ASM)
Diamonds (Kono, Kenema) and gold. ASM formalisation requires electricity.

## 8. Strategic Recommendations

1. Map every Feed Salone investment zone against M300 off-grid coverage
2. Engage mining companies as anchor industrial offtakers for transmission corridors
3. Align M300 policy milestones with EU Budget Support and IMF ECF triggers
4. Use WARDIP2 and SLDTP as co-financiers of energy-for-connectivity infrastructure
5. Present Baomahun energy model as Sierra Leone's mining decarbonisation blueprint
6. Add all complementary programmes to CDMU platform Version 2 economic impact layer
